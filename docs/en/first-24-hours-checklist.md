# First 24 Hours Checklist After a Digital Asset Incident

The first 24 hours should be used to stop further loss, preserve evidence, organize transaction data, and prepare structured materials for exchanges, legal advisors, or law enforcement.

## 0-1 Hour: Stop Further Loss

- Stop sending additional funds.
- Do not pay extra tax, release fee, channel fee, margin, or recovery fee demanded by the counterparty.
- Do not delete chats, wallet records, platform screenshots, or transaction history.
- Do not publicly post full identity information, complete wallet data, or confidential case materials.

## 1-3 Hours: Preserve On-Chain Evidence

Record:

- Victim wallet address
- Suspect recipient address
- Transaction hashes
- Chain name
- Token name
- Amount
- Block timestamp
- Current downstream address, if visible

## 3-6 Hours: Preserve Case Evidence

Save:

- Chat records
- Contact identifiers
- Group links
- Fake platform URLs
- Deposit, balance, and withdrawal-failure screenshots
- Instructions that induced the transfer
- Source-of-funds records

## 6-12 Hours: Write the Incident Timeline

Document:

1. How the counterparty first contacted you
2. How the transfer or signature was induced
3. Each transaction, amount, address, and time
4. When you discovered the issue
5. What the counterparty said afterwards
6. Whether you have contacted an exchange or reported the incident

## 12-24 Hours: Prepare External Materials

Depending on the fund path, prepare:

- Police report materials
- Exchange contact materials
- Stablecoin issuer preservation materials, if applicable
- On-chain fund-flow description
- Source-of-funds explanation
- Identity and account relationship proof

## Important Notice

This checklist is for documentation and incident-response preparation. It is not legal advice and does not guarantee freezing, recovery, account action, or asset return.
